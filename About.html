Our Story

Built to challenge the conventional consulting model.

Meridian was founded in 2008 by four partners who had grown frustrated with the consulting industry they'd spent their careers in. They saw firms that delivered brilliant analysis and then disappeared. They saw clients left holding strategies they couldn't execute and reports they couldn't use.

The founding idea was simple: stay in the room until the work is done. Measure success by what actually changes in the organization, not by what gets presented in the boardroom. Hire people who combine intellectual rigor with genuine humility, and who care more about the client's outcome than their own career advancement.

Sixteen years later, that idea is still the whole firm. We're bigger — 450 people across four continents — but the operating philosophy hasn't changed at all.

Our Values

What we believe.

Not aspirations on a wall. Principles that shape how we hire, how we work, and what we'll turn down.

🔬

Intellectual Honesty

We tell clients what they need to hear — including when the answer is uncomfortable, when the timeline is unrealistic, or when we're not the right firm for the job. Uncomfortable truths early save expensive mistakes later. We've never lost a long-term client relationship by being honest. We've lost a few by being too cautious to say what we really thought.

🤝

Partnership First

Our success is entirely a function of our clients' success. That alignment drives everything — from how we price engagements to how we measure our own performance. We don't optimize for the next piece of work; we optimize for the outcome of this one. The next engagement tends to follow on its own when you get that right.

⚡

Speed with Rigor

The world doesn't wait for perfect analysis. We move fast — but never faster than the evidence allows. Our teams are trained to distinguish between decisions that need more data and decisions that are simply being avoided. Speed isn't about cutting corners; it's about relentlessly focusing on what actually matters to the decision at hand.

🌱

Long-term Thinking

We optimize for our clients' futures, not our next engagement. Sometimes that means recommending against a project that would be profitable for us. It always means being honest about what will and won't work over a 3–5 year horizon, even when it contradicts what leadership wants to hear in the moment.
